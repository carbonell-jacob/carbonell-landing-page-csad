Childhood Years

My name is Jacob Sioson Carbonell. I am currently a 19 year-old Filipino college student living in Parañaque. Looking back at my childhood, I can’t seem to remember a time when I was ever sad. My parents have always told me that when I was young, I was very hyper. It was true, I was always the loudest in the house when I played and messed around. I had a lot of toys gifted to me by my aunt that I would play with every day. My favorite toy is LEGO. I remember building random structures instead of following the guides and spending hours finding the pieces that I needed. I didn’t have access to the internet at a young age. They were against it because they believed it would affect my development. We had a family computer but they put a password on it so I couldn’t use it. The only time I remember I used a computer was when I played snail Bob online with my father. During my elementary school days in St. John Paul II Academy, I don’t remember doing a lot of schoolworks at all. I was a very social person, so instead of studying I spent most of the time hanging out with my friends. This was the reason why I was put into tutoring for multiple subjects by my parents. My favorite subject back then was Christian Living because it was the easiest and the one I was most familiar with since our family is religious. I met two of my closest friends during this time. Sometimes we would talk about our childhood memories in and out of school, reminiscing the fun times that we had together. Childhood felt like I had all the freedom to do whatever I wanted . I don’t remember crying at all, and I was thankful for that because I would remember how truly happy I was.

Teenage Years

I was taught to always be patient and have a positive outlook on life by my parents. They always tell me that I should always serve as a good example to my younger brother. This was when I realized it was time to grow up. I noticed that I started to have responsibilities, feel pressure academically, and become self-aware. When I was around 9th to 10th grade I realized there is more to life than just playing around, I had to be serious now. I started to take academics seriously in junior and senior high school. I disciplined myself to do my work diligently, practiced proper time management, and conditioned my mind and body to become more responsible, all while applying the principles I learned at home. I did not become a top student by doing all of these, in fact, I am just average, but I know that these qualities will help me in the long run. I met new friends during this time, and through this process I learned more about myself. In contrast to my loud and hyper childhood self, I realized I am actually a quiet and introverted person. I also found out I like to play musical instruments such as piano and guitar, and found new hobbies such as drawing, coding, playing video games, cycling, and going to the gym. I experimented with a lot of things to find my interests. I tried all the clubs in school, watched different TV shows and movies genres, and played all kinds of sports such as basketball, table tennis, and badminton. In doing this I found out I like playing basketball, watching sitcoms and anime, and cooking, so I joined the cooking club in my school. Being a teenager, I was not exempted from puberty. It was kind of a difficult time for me because this was the only time I felt a strong feeling of insecurity. When people told me I looked and sounded different, I felt embarrassed and didn’t want to show myself to anyone. I felt really confused and unsafe, but thankfully my parents helped and supported me throughout these times. Overall, my teenage years are an integral part of my life. This is when I started exploring different things and finding myself in the process

College Years

After senior high school, it was time to apply for college. I applied to three universities, Far Eastern University Alabang, Polytechnic University of the Philippines, and University of the East Manila. I remember studying for the entrance exams for sleepless nights, living off of coffee. After finding out that I passed all of them, I remembered being so happy it paid off. When I began studying in UE Manila, I was overwhelmed by how different it is to my previous school. The culture of this university is deeper and more diverse, the campus is bigger, and there are way more students. The learning environment in college is also different compared to what I was used to. I realized that I am not studying to excel in my grades anymore, I study to survive There are times when it gets really exhausting and stressful, but I just try my best to remind myself that as long as I do my best, everything will be fine. The overwhelming amount of work is an opportunity for me to improve my time and stress management. I realized that I have to adjust for a while. In my first two weeks, I found my circle of friends, and I am happy that I am still with them now. College wouldn’t be complete without them because they serve as my support group. To not get burned out, I try to balance my academic and personal life. However, I still struggle sometimes because I don’t know how to do it properly. Overall, college has been a fun ride so far. There are times where I feel lost and overwhelmed, but I know that everything I experience here has a purpose. It was never going to be an easy journey in the first place. Computer science has thrown different kinds of challenges at me, and I know it will continue to do so in the next 2 years. I just hope that by then, I will face it as a more well-rounded individual.
